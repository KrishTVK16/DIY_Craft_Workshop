5 Easy Steps to Start Pottery at Home

Jan 29, 2026 • Sarah Jenkins

Pottery is one of the most rewarding crafts you can learn. It teaches patience, focus, and gives you a tangible connection to the earth. But many people think you need a giant kiln and wheel to get started. That's simply not true!

With modern air-dry clays, you can create stunning pieces right at your kitchen table. Here are 5 steps to get started.

1. Choose the Right Clay

Start with a high-quality air-dry clay reinforced with nylon fibers. This prevents cracking as your piece dries.

2. Prepare Your Workspace

You don't need a studio. A simple wooden board or a piece of canvas on your table works perfectly. This prevents the clay from sticking.

3. Learn the Pinch Pot Method

The oldest and simplest technique. Roll a ball of clay, stick your thumb in, and gently pinch the walls to shape a bowl.

4. Smoothing and Refining

Use a damp sponge to smooth out any fingerprints. The smoother your piece before drying, the better it will look painted.

5. Painting and Sealing

Once dry (wait 24-48 hours), use acrylic paints to decorate. Finish with a waterproof varnish to make it durable.
